# 01 — Hackathon Rules & What They Expect

Source: kanadshield.com (Problem Statement pages T&C, How It Works, Timeline).
All Category 2 problem statements share the same Terms & Conditions.

---

## The Process (5 Steps)

1. **Explore Problem Statement** — browse policing-category challenges.
2. **Click "Apply Now"** — **no login required**.
3. **Submission** — upload solution, prototype details, and supporting documents.
4. **Pitch at Hackathon Event** — present **live to experts & government officials**.
5. **Results & Rewards** — recognition and prizes.

> Implication: This is a **submission-first** model. You submit online by 20 June, then
> (if shortlisted/invited) you pitch live at i-Hub Gujarat. The live pitch to police +
> govt officials is where compliance-depth wins.

---

## Team Composition & Eligibility

- **Team size:** minimum **2**, maximum **6** members.
- **Exceptions:** more than 6 needs prior written approval (email support@kanadshield.com).
- **Single participation:** one person can be in only one team.
- **Category 2 eligibility:** Graduates, Post Graduates, doctoral students, other technical
  courses. (We qualify as engineering students.)

---

## Registration & Deadlines

- **Submission deadline:** **20 June 2026, 11:59 PM**.
- **Late entries:** anything after 11:59 PM on the deadline is **not considered**. No exceptions.

---

## Problem Statement Submission Rules

- **Submission limit:** a single team may submit a maximum of **5 problem statements**.
  (We are doing 3 — well within limit.)
- **Relevance:** submissions must be strictly related to the problem statement chosen.

---

## Mandatory Deliverables (DISQUALIFICATION RISK)

These are not optional. Missing them can disqualify you outright:

- **Presentation** — must be demoable, with in-depth accurate info about the problem and
  the intended solution.
- **Abstract** — concise: proposed solution, methodology, and tools to be used.
- **Documentation** — failure to produce a presentation or abstract at the evaluation phase
  **may lead to immediate disqualification.**

> Build the abstract + presentation EARLY, not the night before. They are gating items.

---

## Code of Conduct

- **Ethical hacking only.** Disrupting event infra or unauthorized access to third-party
  systems = permanent ban + potential legal action.
- **Originality.** All submitted work must be original. Plagiarism of code or concepts from
  existing proprietary software is strictly prohibited.

> Implication for us: our LazyCook-style validation pipeline and VERONICA/PRISM-derived
> architecture are **our own prior work** — fine to build on. But any legal datasets must be
> from legitimate public/licensed sources (Indian Kanoon API, indiacode.nic.in), credited.

---

## What "Good" Looks Like To These Judges

Synthesized from the evaluation criteria across both our PSs:

1. **Real-world applicability** appears in nearly every PS. They want deployable, not toy.
2. **Police integration & real-time response** is weighted heavily.
3. **Quality and admissibility of digital evidence** — this is a recurring, explicit criterion.
4. **Data privacy & security compliance** — explicitly scored.
5. **Innovation** — but anchored to a real policing pain point, not novelty for its own sake.
6. **Usability for non-technical law enforcement officers** — clean, simple UI matters.

> The pattern: they reward **practical, compliant, demoable** systems that a real officer
> could use — far more than algorithmic cleverness.
