# Research — POCSO Act + IT Act: Grooming & Mandatory Reporting

> The Child Protection PS demands "grooming detection" and "predator identification."
> That triggers POCSO + IT Act duties. The mandatory-reporting rule turns our
> "report to police" feature from a nice-to-have into a LEGAL OBLIGATION built into the product.

---

## POCSO Act, 2012 — Essentials

- Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act. **Child = under 18.** Gender-neutral.
- Covers penetrative/non-penetrative assault, sexual harassment, and pornography (CSAM).
- **Section 11** — sexual harassment (interpreted to cover grooming).
- **Section 12** — punishes sexual harassment including via electronic/online means
  (sexually coloured remarks, showing pornographic material, repeatedly contacting a child
  with sexual intent).
- **Sections 13–15** — use of a child in media for sexual gratification / CSAM offences.
- **Section 14** — ≥5 years imprisonment + fine (first offence); ≥7 years (subsequent).

## The Mandatory Reporting Rule (BIG for our product)

- **Section 19** — it is **mandatory** to report sexual offences against children.
- Any person (doctor, teacher, parent, professional) who suspects or witnesses an offence
  must report to police, in practice **within 24 hours**.
- **Failure to report** is punishable by imprisonment up to **6 months** or fine.

> PRODUCT IMPLICATION: When our AI flags grooming/abuse risk, the system must FACILITATE
> reporting to the Cyber Crime Branch. Frame this as "POCSO Section 19 mandatory-reporting
> compliance built-in." Killer line in a police pitch.

## IT Act Interplay (online grooming)

- **Section 67B, IT Act** — criminalises online grooming (building a relationship with a
  child online to facilitate sexual contact) and CSAM.
- **Section 77, IT Act** — allows concurrent application of other penal laws, so an offender
  can be prosecuted under both IT Act and POCSO.
- Typical mapping: initial grooming communication → IT Act s.67B; soliciting explicit images
  / video calls → POCSO s.11 + s.13–15.

## IT Rules 2021 (Intermediary Guidelines) — Recent Tightening

- **Rule 3(2)(b)** — intermediary must remove/disable access to flagged intimate/CSAM-type
  content (incl. morphed images) within **2 hours** of a complaint in some cases; CSAM
  generally within **24 hours** of complaint or court order.
- **Amendments came into force 20 February 2026** strengthening these safeguards — cite this
  as evidence our solution is aligned with the *latest* regulatory posture.

## Real Government Systems To Reference

- **Childline 1098** — 24-hour child-distress helpline.
- **Cyber Crime Helpline 155260** — for online exploitation reporting.
- **TrackChild** (Ministry of Women & Child Development) — the actual missing-child database;
  CCTNS integrates with it. This is our "missing child database" integration target.
- **NCRB** — maintains child-offence crime data (crime-in-india).
- **ITSSO** — Investigation Tracking System for Sexual Offences (law-enforcement module on CCTNS).
